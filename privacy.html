Privacy policy

Privacy-first means the data story stays explicit.

Last updated: March 6, 2026

1. Scope

This policy applies to the Notisaver Android application and this static website. The website is informational and does not run analytics or collect account data by default.

2. Data the app may process

Notisaver is designed to process notification metadata and content shown on the device, including app name, package name, title, text, timestamps, and related notification details needed to display history and conversation views.

3. How that data is used

The captured data is used to archive notifications, support search and filters, generate summaries, organize conversations, and create exports or backups when the user requests them.

4. Local storage model

Notification history is intended to remain on the device unless the user initiates a backup, export, or sharing action. Users stay in control of deletion, exclusion rules, and retention behavior.

5. Permissions

The app may require notification access and other platform permissions that are directly tied to app features. These permissions are used only for the features the user enables.

6. Backup and export

When users export data or create backups, that information can leave the app sandbox by design. Users should handle exported files carefully and store them only in trusted locations.

7. Security practices

Notisaver is presented as a privacy-focused product with support for app lock, local controls, and encrypted backups using AES-256-GCM for supported backup flows.

8. User controls

Users can mute specific apps, remove stored records, export their data, enable or disable lock features, and stop using the service at any time through Android settings or app controls.

9. Children and sensitive use

Notisaver is not directed to children. Users should avoid storing or exporting sensitive material unless they understand the privacy and storage implications on their own device.

10. Contact and updates

If you have privacy questions, contact peakliterature@gmail.com. Material policy updates will be reflected on this page with a new effective date.
